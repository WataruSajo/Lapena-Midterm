History Of Batangas

Originally comprised of Occidental Mindoro, Oriental Mindoro, Marinduque, and parts of Laguna and Ambos-Camarines, Batangas was known as COUMINTAN or KUMINTANG. In 1534, it became the first practically organized province in Luzon, officially founded in 1581. Balayan was the capital from 1597-1732 before it moved to Taal. After severe eruptions of Taal Volcano, the capital was transferred to Batangas City in 1754, where it remains today.
